Introduction

We construct a new real-scene dataset named CDR (Categorized, Diverse, Real-world) that is an order of magnitude larger than existing real-world datasets for reflection removal. Our dataset consists of ground-truth reflection, transmission, and mixture images in both RAW and RGB formats with diverse glass types and environments.

Motivation

  • To reduce the domain gap between the benchmark and general real scenes.
  • To provide perfect alignment and small intensity difference.
  • To use a diverse set of glass in our daily life to capture different reflections.

This dataset is collected mainly by three cameras: a DSLR (Digital Single-Lens Reflex) camera Canon EOS 50D, a MILC (Mirrorless Interchangeable Lens Cameras) Nikon Z6, and a smartphone camera Huawei Mate30. In total, 1063 image triple-sets (M, R, T) are provided. The resolution for each image triple-set is different. In each triple-set, we provide a mixed image M, a reflection image R, and a transmission image T. Both RAW data and RGB data are provided in our dataset. We sort the images according to different criteria, including the smoothness of reflection and transmission, the relative intensity of reflection and transmission, and the ghosting effect of reflection.
